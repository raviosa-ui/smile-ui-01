Real-life usage scenarios

It is frequently used when announcing a new job, closing a big business deal, buying a house, or celebrating New Year's Eve.

Friends use it to hype each other up before a glamorous night out, a wedding reception, or a weekend trip.

It is also a favorite for sports fans reacting to a championship win.

How this emoji affects tone and emotion

This emoji adds a highly energetic, premium, and victorious tone to any announcement.

It elevates a simple 'congratulations' into a grand, high-energy celebratory cheer.

Professional vs casual usage

In casual chats, it is perfect for hyping up big life events and weekend plans.

In the workplace, it is heavily used on internal platforms like Slack to celebrate a team hitting a major sales goal or launching a project. However, it should still be avoided in strict, formal corporate emails to clients.

When to use

Use this emoji when celebrating major achievements, milestones, victories, or luxurious events.

When not to use

Avoid using it in serious, sensitive, or non-celebratory discussions.

Common mistakes and misuse

Because it explicitly depicts an alcoholic beverage, using it in messages involving minors, recovery, or sober events can be inappropriate or insensitive.

It can also seem overly boastful or dramatic if used to celebrate a very minor, everyday task (like finishing the laundry).
