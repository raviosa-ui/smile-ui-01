Professional vs casual usage

In casual settings, it is a top-tier emoji for showing respect and agreement among friends.

In professional settings, it is a bit too 'bro-culture' for most emails, though it can work in highly relaxed, close-knit startup environments.

When to use

Use this to agree with a friend, show support and solidarity, celebrate a team victory, or playfully threaten someone.

When not to use

Avoid using it in serious or heated arguments where a 'punch' emoji could be misinterpreted as an actual threat of violence.

Common mistakes and misuse

Using it out of context with someone you do not know well can feel oddly aggressive, as it looks like a punch rather than a greeting if the relationship isn't established.
