Real-life usage scenarios

Constantly used as a sign-off in text messages among friends.

How this emoji affects tone and emotion

This emoji adds a very cool, unbothered, and friendly tone to a message.

It keeps the conversation feeling lighthearted and removes any pressure or formality.

Professional vs casual usage

In casual settings, it is a perfect, universally understood friendly gesture.

In professional settings, it is usually too casual for clients, but completely acceptable when saying goodbye to a friendly coworker on Friday afternoon.

When to use

Use this to say a casual goodbye, express a relaxed and positive attitude, or indicate the number two.

When not to use

Avoid using it in highly formal business emails where a professional sign-off (like 'Best regards') is expected instead of a casual 'peace out'.

Common mistakes and misuse

Using it to respond to someone who is genuinely angry with you can seem highly dismissive, as if you are saying 'whatever, I'm out.'
