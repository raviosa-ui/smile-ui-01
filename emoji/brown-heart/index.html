How this emoji affects tone and emotion

This emoji adds a very grounded, warm, and secure tone to a message.

It feels less 'loud' than a red or pink heart, making it feel very sincere and stable.

Professional vs casual usage

In casual settings, it is a beautiful, warm way to show support.

In professional settings, hearts are usually avoided, but it may appear in marketing for coffee brands or autumn-themed campaigns.

When to use

Use this to show support for communities of color, to express a warm, grounded friendship, or to caption photos of coffee and autumn landscapes.

When not to use

There are very few wrong ways to use this heart, but avoid using it if you are specifically trying to convey a traditional, highly romantic Valentine's Day message.

Common mistakes and misuse

It is rarely misused, though using it to describe something dirty or gross simply because of the color is generally frowned upon.
