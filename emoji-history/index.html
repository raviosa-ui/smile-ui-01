The Origin of the Smiley

How a simple yellow circle became the world's most popular language. From 1963 to 2026.

Part I: The Analog Era

The Story of the "Smiley"

Before digital emojis, we had the physical Smiley. Here is how your favorite yellow face was born.

1963

The 10-Minute Sketch

Commercial artist Harvey Ball was hired by an insurance company to boost employee morale. He drew a yellow circle with two dots and a smile in just 10 minutes. He was paid $45 for the design. He never trademarked it!

1982

The First Emoticon :-)

Computer scientist Scott Fahlman realized online message boards were confusing sarcasm with seriousness. He suggested using :-) for jokes and :-( for serious posts. The "Emoticon" was born.

Part II: The Digital Era

Emoji Origins in Japan (1999)

The word "Emoji" comes from Japanese: e (picture) + moji (character).

1999

176 Pixels

Shigetaka Kurita created the first set of 176 emojis for NTT DoCoMo pagers. They were tiny 12x12 pixel grids used to show the weather ‚õÖ and traffic.

2010

Global Standard

Unicode officially adopted emojis. This meant a smiley sent from an iPhone would finally show up on an Android phone. Emojis became a global language.
