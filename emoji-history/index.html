The Origin of the Smiley

How a simple yellow circle became the world's most popular language. From 1963 to 2026.

The Complete Timeline

From Analog to Digital

The journey of the smiley face spans over 60 years. Here are the 5 most critical moments in history.

1963

The $45 Sketch

Commercial artist Harvey Ball was hired by State Mutual Life Assurance to boost employee morale. He drew a yellow circle with two dots and a smile in just 10 minutes. He was paid $45 and never trademarked the design.

1972

The Smiley Brand

French journalist Franklin Loufrani trademarked the Smiley face to highlight good news in the newspaper France Soir. He launched "The Smiley Company," turning the symbol into a global licensing empire.

1982

The First Emoticon :-)

Computer scientist Scott Fahlman at Carnegie Mellon University realized online text sarcasm was confusing. He posted a message suggesting :-) for jokes and :-( for serious matters.

1999

The Birth of "Emoji"

Shigetaka Kurita created the first set of 176 emojis for Japanese mobile carrier NTT DoCoMo. They were tiny 12x12 pixel grids used to show information like weather ‚õÖ and traffic.

2010

Unicode Adoption

Emojis were officially added to the Unicode Standard. This was the turning point that allowed an iPhone user to send a smiley to an Android user and have it display correctly. Emojis became a global language.
